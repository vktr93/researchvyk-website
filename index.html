Professional Summary

I am an interdisciplinary Ph.D. scientist with over five years of experience at the intersection of research and development, specializing in DNA nanotechnology, materials science, and advanced sensor fabrication. My career has been driven by a passion for solving complex problems, leading research projects from their initial concept to peer-reviewed publication, patenting, and securing competitive international funding like the JSPS Fellowship.

As an adaptable and creative problem-solver, I have eagerly embraced emerging technologies. I have a proven track record of directing generative AI to architect and deploy sophisticated applications, including a multi-agent system for automated scientific peer-review. My goal is to apply my deep scientific background and technical project management skills to address significant challenges in a research or technology-focused environment.

DNA-FET for High-Speed Data Retrieval

Lead Researcher & Inventor

Developed and fabricated a proof-of-concept DNA Field-Effect Transistor (FET) for a novel electronic method of data location in DNA data storage. This foundational work led to a first-author publication and a Japanese patent application.

Lead Researcher & Inventor
